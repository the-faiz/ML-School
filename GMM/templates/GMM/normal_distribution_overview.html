1D normal Distribution

A random variable is said to be normally distributed if pdf of the variables follows below equation \begin{align} p(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt 2πσ^2}*e^-\frac{(x-μ)^2}{2σ^2} \end{align} \( \mu= \) mean of the distribution
\( \sigma = \) standard deviation of the distribution
\( \mu , \sigma \) are called parameters of the distribution and they control center and spread of the distribution.

2D Normal Distribution

Pdf of 2D normal distribution is given by following equation \begin{align} p(x)=\frac{1}{2\pi * | \Sigma |^\frac{1}{2}}*exp\{\frac{1}{2}*(x-\mu)^T\Sigma^{-1}(x-\mu)\} \end{align} There are two parameters in this equation

  • \( \mu \) which is called the mean vector and determines the center of the distribution.
  • \( \Sigma \) which is called covariance matrix and controls the shape and width of the distribution

How Gaussian Mixture Models learns the shape of clusters

As we have seen above, the shape and orientation of normal distribution is controlled by the covariance matrix. GMM tries to fit Normal distributions to the clusters and there are two parameters required to fit normal distributions to each of the clusters.
  • \( \mu \) vector for each of the clusters. GMM learns these vectors during training. These vectors define the centers of each of the clusters.
  • \( \Sigma \), covariance matrix for each of the clusters. This is also learned during model training. Depending upon learned covariance matrix shapes and orientation of the clusters would be different.
